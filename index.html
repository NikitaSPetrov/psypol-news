There’s Only One War
There’s Only One War

When I was little, WW1 and WW2 seemed like discrete events from wholly different eras, separated and surrounded by peace. That’s not what they were.

There were 21 years in between them — the distance from now to 2005 (the year when the radio played Feel Good Inc. by Gorillaz, Mr. Brightside by The Killers, and Hollaback Girl by Gwen Stefani). These years were filled with civil wars, revolutions, and border conflicts.

The end of WW2 was also the start of the Cold War (which was not all that cold for Vietnam or Korea). We say that it ended with the fall of the Soviet Union, but the news from Ukraine suggests otherwise.

The last century is a continuous flow of violence.

Maybe there’s just one war going round and round and round, and local escalations in different regions of time and space are like solar flares or storms in the ocean — not in any way separate from the currents around them.

Discussed in: The Nonzero Podcast, “Will the Iran War Spiral Out of Control?” — Nikita Petrov & Robert Wright

PARALYSIS & HALLUCINATIONS

I watch from above two lives play out in a sequence, and I hear a continuous, iterative articulation of these lives’ methods and essences. Maybe 80% is indistinct, a kind of glossolalia, but occasionally words and phrases emerge out of it. It sounds a little like chanting and works a little like writing a song: a magic search for meaning by touch, through humming, mumbling, stumbling towards something true, a closer and closer articulation of what these lives I am watching play out are expressions of, the principle behind them.

The first life is of a man who builds a house and makes it a home for him and his woman. They share what seems like a good, harmonious life. Later the wife dies, and he carries on, kind of stoic, alone, in the now empty-home.

The second is of a giant tree, like a sequoia, that leads an unrushed, majestic existence for a very long time and eventually gets cut down. The stump is made into a beautiful house, then home, still carrying the tree’s essence further.

I watch this, and it’s resonant. Both lives have a lot of beauty and a kind of a grace, strength, steadiness, dignity. There are things in life you accept with humility and reverence (like birth, death, heartache, deep love). These lives are like this. I watch it all like a David Lynch movie, a Buddhist khural, or a psychedelic vision — it speaks to me, I sense that it’s saying something true about reality. The more I see, the more the audible formulations move to higher levels of abstraction, expressing something more universally true about the two lives and about life itself.

I don’t remember the phrases, but the visions feel so compelling I almost can’t help but claim them as my other lives — past, or adjacent — a mythological or archetypal dimension of the same process that my waking life is a dimension of.

And it’s not threatening. It’s like the way I relate to Tibetan practices or to writing: a reality emerges from my mind and soul, I observe and relate to it, I experience it, and it’s like what I’ve been experiencing, something I’ve always sensed about life. It feels good.

I wake up in a good mood, with a touch of a kind of a cheerful, humorous wisdom.

PARALYSIS & HALLUCINATIONS

I realize I am dreaming, for the first time in a while. To my surprise and excitement, lucidity neither fades away nor wakes me up, and I start thinking about the best way to use this opportunity.

My wife has a suggestion: the sink is leaking, and now would be a great time to fix it.

I try to explain that home improvements done in a dream do not translate into the waking world; her plan would lead to me spending an unusually stable lucid dream on plumbing, only to have to do it all over again upon waking up.

She shrugs: if something having to do with dreams prevents me from doing it now, in the morning would be fine too. It’s just it needs to happen sometime soon.

I’m not too happy with how this is working out, but I decide to focus on maintaining awareness and intentionality. Ok, I say, I’ll go wake up and be out in a minute. I walk back into the bedroom and lie down. I focus my intention and do wake up in the everyday world.

I do NOT get up right away and soon end up falling back to sleep. When I do leave the bedroom, I do not start working on the sink. It’s not leaking most of the time, so even diagnosing the problem would take time and effort, and K’s waking-world version doesn’t seem to be bothered by it, she’s not mentioning it to me at all.

(Next month I get a plumber to replace the faucet.)

As often happens in such dreams, I remember this had happened before and suspect that I might be dreaming. I even remember a tip I recently heard: that reading or using a calculator is impossible in a dream, so trying to do that is a great way to do a reality-check.

I manage to procure a book, and then a calculator, but the hack doesn’t work: after opening the book, instead of trying to read, I just assess whether the words and the letters look right, and they seem fine at a glance; the digits on the calculator do not look ok, they’re all jumbled, but some character pops out from the other room to explain that this is a Chinese calculator, it uses a different coding or some such — and then another tooth falls out, which is very distressing and distracting, and I spend the rest of the night non-lucid.
