There’s Only One War
There’s Only One War

When I was little, WW1 and WW2 seemed like discrete events from wholly different eras, separated and surrounded by peace. That’s not what they were.

There were 21 years in between them — the distance from now to 2005 (the year when the radio played Feel Good Inc. by Gorillaz, Mr. Brightside by The Killers, and Hollaback Girl by Gwen Stefani). These years were filled with civil wars, revolutions, and border conflicts.

The end of WW2 was also the start of the Cold War (which was not all that cold for Vietnam or Korea). We say that it ended with the fall of the Soviet Union, but the news from Ukraine suggests otherwise.

The last century is a continuous flow of violence.

Maybe there’s just one war going round and round and round, and local escalations in different regions of time and space are like solar flares or storms in the ocean — not in any way separate from the currents around them.
