One War
One War

There were only 21 years between the end of WW1 and the start of WW2. These years were filled with civil wars, revolutions, and border conflicts which were linked causally to both world wars — which themselves were made of many smaller wars that did not all start and end at the same time.

So treating them, like we do, as discrete events separated by a stretch of peace is no more than a convention. It would be equally accurate to talk about one war lasting between 1914 and 1945.

Except, the end of WW2 was also the start of the Cold War (which was not all that cold for Vietnam or Korea). We say that the latter ended with the fall of the Soviet Union, but the news from Ukraine suggests otherwise.

The flow of violence has been uninterrupted in the past 100 years. In some periods, it was more like a giant waterfall, and in others, like a thousand smaller rivers. But it never dried up. And if we look at the wars preceding and leading up to WW1, we’ll see that it did not originate in the 20th century, either.

Perhaps there is just one war, going round and round, and local escalations in different regions of time and space are like solar flares or storms in the ocean — not in any way separate from the currents around them.

Discussed in: The Nonzero Podcast, “Will the Iran War Spiral Out of Control?” — Nikita Petrov & Robert Wright
Alienism
Alienism

UFOs, DMT entities, and AI all deal with something similar: encounters with non-human intelligence that, depending on how you look at them, can seem like a nothing-burger (UFOs aren’t real, DMT is hallucinations, LLMs are “stochastic parrots”) or as reality-changing (aliens have been guiding human evolution, DMT is a portal to other realities inhabited by powerful beings, AI is taking over the world and may be conscious).

The gap between these two readings is enormous, and there is no consensus on which is closer to the truth. Alienism is the study of this gap — and of the recurring pattern of entity encounters across radically different domains.
